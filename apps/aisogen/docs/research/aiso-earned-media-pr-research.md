## Executive Summary

Earned media is the single highest-impact layer for AI search visibility. Across the major AI models, 82–95% of all citations come from non-paid sources, with earned media (journalism, third-party coverage, directories) dominating. Wikipedia alone accounts for 7.8% of all ChatGPT citations. The key insight from 2025 research: **brand search volume, not backlinks, is now the strongest predictor of AI citations** (0.334 correlation per Ahrefs, December 2025). This fundamentally reframes how earned media should be measured and executed.

**The core tension for a SaaS product:** This layer is the hardest to control and automate. Most competitor tools (Profound, Otterly, ZipTie) only _monitor_ earned media citations — they do not _generate_ them. The gap in the market is an automated earned media _execution_ layer, not just a measurement layer.

## 1. Wikipedia for AI Visibility

### Why Wikipedia Matters

Wikipedia is ChatGPT's single most cited source at 7.8% of all citations (analysis of 680 million citations, August 2024 – June 2025). Across all LLMs, it ranks second only to Reddit in aggregate citation frequency. It functions as a "truth anchor" — AI models treat Wikipedia content as validated ground truth.

**Citation lag:** Wikipedia changes propagate to ChatGPT in approximately 14–28 days via real-time retrieval (grounding), separate from the model's training cutoff. The mechanism is both:

- **Training data** — Wikipedia dumps are included in most LLM pre-training corpora
- **Real-time retrieval** — ChatGPT and Perplexity actively retrieve Wikipedia content at query time

Companies _with_ Wikipedia articles achieved first AI citations within **28 days** on average. Companies _without_ Wikipedia presence took **52 days** on average.

### Notability Requirements (WP:GNG + WP:CORP)

Wikipedia's General Notability Guideline (WP:GNG) requires that a subject has received **significant coverage** in **multiple independent, reliable secondary sources**. For companies and organisations (WP:CORP), the standard is deliberately strict to prevent marketing abuse.

What counts:

- Coverage in national newspapers, mainstream magazines, or major industry publications
- In-depth articles (not passing mentions or product listings)
- Multiple independent sources (not all from the same publisher)

What does not count:

- Press releases (even if republished)
- Company website content
- Trade directory listings
- Advertising features or sponsored content
- A single source, regardless of quality
- Coverage in niche trade journals too closely affiliated with the industry

**Realistic threshold for a mid-market brand:** 3–5 in-depth articles in publications with DR 70+ that are genuinely independent. For Hairgenetix or Love Over Exile, this means national beauty/health press or mainstream media — not just trade press.

### Wikipedia Editing Rules and Ethics

Wikipedia requires **paid contribution disclosure** (Wikimedia Foundation Terms of Use, amended 2014). Any editor receiving payment to edit must disclose their employer, client, and affiliation. Undisclosed paid editing is a violation that can result in:

- Permanent block of the editor account
- Deletion of the article
- Negative press coverage of the brand

The recommended approach for brands is:

1. Build the notability through earned coverage first (correct sequence)
2. Create a Wikipedia article only after meeting WP:GNG thresholds
3. Use the article's Talk page to suggest corrections as a disclosed COI editor
4. Hire a legitimate Wikipedia consultant (e.g., Beutler Ink) for complex situations — they work within Wikipedia's guidelines

**Risk:** Editors actively monitor for promotional language and COI. New articles from unestablished accounts are scrutinised heavily.

### Tools for Wikipedia Monitoring

- **WikiDashboard** — tracks edit history and editor patterns on any article
- **Wikipedia Watch List** — email notifications for changes to specific pages
- **Five Blocks** — specialist firm for brand Wikipedia management and monitoring
- **Status Labs** — offers Wikipedia strategy within broader AI reputation management

### Recommendations

| -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |

## 3. News Coverage and Press

### How AI Models Weight News Articles

From Muck Rack's study (earned media research, September 2025):

- 95% of all AI citations come from unpaid media sources
- 85% of those unpaid citations are earned media (journalism, third-party coverage)
- 50% of all AI model responses included at least one earned media citation
- Journalism accounts for approximately 20–30% of citations in any given period

**Critical finding:** When Muck Rack compared the most-pitched journalists on their platform with those most cited by AI engines for the same brands, the overlap was only **2%** on average. PR teams are pitching the wrong journalists for AI visibility.

4. **Clear entity identification** — Brand name, product name, founder name in structured context
5. **Publication authority** — AI models strongly favour DR 70+ publications

## 5. Podcast and Interview Mentions

### How Podcast Mentions Feed AI Models

Per Rephonic's research (2025) and Francesca Tabor's analysis ("Audio Is the New Dataset," July 2025):

LLMs are trained on text transcripts — the audio itself is not directly ingested, but transcripts, show notes, and episode summaries published on the open web are. Key mechanisms:

1. **Published transcripts** — If a podcast publishes full transcripts (Rev, Descript), these become crawlable content
2. **Show notes with brand mentions** — Indexed by search engines and LLMs
3. **YouTube episode uploads** — YouTube is cited by Perplexity and Google AI; video descriptions with brand mentions matter
4. **Guest bio pages** — Podcast host pages listing guests are crawlable
5. **Spotify/Apple descriptions** — Limited crawlability but growing

**Correlation finding (Ahrefs, December 2025):** YouTube mentions correlate strongly with AI visibility, alongside brand search volume. YouTube is cited by Perplexity and Google AI Overviews.

### Podcast Booking Strategy for AI Authority

Target podcasts that:

1. Publish full text transcripts on their website (not just audio)
2. Upload episodes to YouTube (for Perplexity and Google AI citation)
3. Have show notes that include guest bio and brand mentions with links
4. Have DR 50+ for their main website

Priority categories for Hairgenetix:

- Hair loss / trichology podcasts (niche authority)
- Beauty and wellness podcasts (broad reach, Byrdie/Allure audience overlap)
- Dermatology / skin health podcasts (clinical credibility for AI citations)
- Entrepreneurship podcasts (for founder authority building)

### Recommendations

## 7. Backlink Strategies for AI

### Have Backlinks Changed in the AI Era?

The relationship is more nuanced than either "backlinks are dead" or "backlinks are everything":

**What hasn't changed:**

- High-quality editorial backlinks from DR 70+ publications still correlate with Google rankings
- Google rankings correlate with AI Overview citations (Google AI Overviews favour sources that already rank)
- Digital PR for backlinks and digital PR for AI citations use essentially the same tactics

**What has changed:**

- Brand mentions without links now carry significant weight for AI citation (AI models don't need a link to cite a brand)
- Co-citation patterns matter — appearing alongside credible brands in the same content trains AI associations
- Brand search volume (0.334 correlation with AI citations) outperforms backlink count as a predictor
- Topical relevance of backlinks matters more than domain authority alone for AI visibility

**What Google is penalising (August 2025 Spam Update):**

- Private Blog Networks (PBNs)
- Manipulative link schemes
- Mass AI-generated content
- Rising referring domains with falling organic traffic (devalued by SpamBrain)

### Digital PR as the Convergent Strategy

Digital PR is the single strategy that simultaneously builds:

1. Editorial backlinks (for Google rankings → AI Overview citations)
2. Brand mentions without links (for AI citation training)
3. Press coverage (earned media for AI citation)
4. Entity authority (repeated brand/product name mentions in context)

   48.6% of SEO experts named digital PR the most effective link-building tactic in 2025.

### Recommendations

| Action                                                                          | Priority | Notes                                                                        |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Prioritise digital PR campaigns that generate both links AND brand mentions     | High     | Every campaign should target both signals                                    |
| Create data-driven studies or original research as PR assets                    | High     | Statistics increase AI citations by 22%; original data gets cited repeatedly |
| Focus on contextual relevance — get mentions in content about your exact topics | High     | AI models weight topical alignment heavily                                   |
| Unlinked mention monitoring — track and request links to existing citations     | Medium   | Converts existing AI-citation value into backlink value                      |
| Avoid any PBN or link scheme tactics entirely                                   | High     | August 2025 Spam Update has sharp detection                                  |

## 11. The AI Citation Economy — Latest Research

### How AI Models Select Sources

From Princeton GEO study (KDD 2024), Muck Rack research (2025), and Goodie citation analysis (5.7M citations, 2025):

**Model-specific citation logic:**

| AI Model   | Primary Signal                               | Secondary Signal             | Avoid                        |
| ---------- | -------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| ChatGPT    | Domain authority (DR 70+)                    | Listing and database sources | Low-DR, single-source claims |
| Gemini     | Brand-managed sources (52.1%)                | Google ecosystem signals     | Non-Google-indexed content   |
| Perplexity | Freshness, explicit citations within content | Academic and news sources    | Undated or stale content     |
| Claude     | Directory and database sources (65%)         | Multi-source consensus       | Single-source claims         |

**Universal citation signals:**

1. Multi-source corroboration — the same claim appearing in multiple trusted sources
2. Statistics and quantified claims — increases citation likelihood by 22% (Princeton)
3. Named expert quotes — increases citation likelihood by 37% (Princeton)
4. Entity clarity — brand name, product name, founder name explicitly and consistently named
5. Content recency — 65% of LLM citations target content from the past 12 months
6. First-paragraph placement — 44.2% of citations from the opening 30% of text

**Brand search volume correlation:**
Ahrefs research (December 2025) found brand search volume has a 0.334 correlation with AI citations — stronger than any backlink metric. This means brand building activities (PR, advertising, word of mouth) now directly improve AI visibility, creating a new feedback loop.

**The 11% overlap finding:**
Only 11% of domains are cited by both ChatGPT and Perplexity, confirming that model-specific citation strategies are necessary. A single universal strategy will not achieve visibility across all models.

### FTC and ASA Compliance

- Any sponsored content, paid placements, or compensated reviews must be disclosed per FTC guidelines (updated 2025)
- FTC penalties: up to $51,744 per violation
- Paid links in sponsored articles must use `rel="nofollow"` or `rel="sponsored"` — Google still penalises paid link schemes regardless of nofollowing
- AI-generated testimonials or endorsements require explicit disclosure as AI-created content (FTC 2025 AI guidelines)

### Google Penalties

- August 2025 Spam Update specifically targets manipulative link building, PBNs, and mass AI content
- SpamBrain now detects "rising referring domains + falling organic traffic" as a penalty signal
- Safe tactics: digital PR, genuine editorial coverage, content-based link earning, directory listings
- Unsafe tactics: link exchanges, PBNs, paid links without disclosure, comment spam

### Ethical Considerations

- Wikipedia's neutrality policies exist to protect information quality — brand manipulation erodes the source that AI models rely on most
- HARO/journalist platform spam (mass AI-generated responses) has already degraded platform quality and is filtering out AI responses
- Earned media must be genuinely earned — tactics that fabricate credibility undermine the entire authority-building purpose
