## 1. Context and Why This Matters {#1-context}

### The Shift That Makes This Critical

- Google AI Overviews now appear in **84% of search results** (Position Digital, 2025)
- **92% of search interactions** now happen within AI-generated responses
- Brands cited in AI Overviews earn **35% more organic clicks** and **91% more paid clicks**
- AI traffic (currently 0.13% of sessions) is concentrated on **high-intent pages** and growing at 3.26x year-over-year
- ChatGPT owns **84.2%** of AI referrals. Copilot grew 25.2x. Claude grew 12.8x (Previsible, 2025)

### The Multimodal Premium

Multi-modal content combining text, images, video, and structured data sees **156% higher selection rates** in Google AI Overviews. Full multimodal plus schema integration delivers up to **317% more citations**. This single statistic is the most important driver for a multi-channel distribution strategy.

Source: [Google AI Overviews Ranking Factors 2026 — Wellows](https://wellows.com/blog/google-ai-overviews-ranking-factors/) — analysis of 15,847 AI Overview results across 63 industries.

### What AI Models Actually Look For

Before examining channels, the core selection logic applies to all of them:

- **Risk minimisation over relevance** — AI asks "what's the safest thing I can repeat without being wrong"
- **Novel statistics** boost AI visibility by **41%** (GEO peer-reviewed study)
- **Quotable expert findings** boost citation rate by **37%**
- **Brand search volume** is the strongest predictor of LLM citations (correlation: 0.334) — outweighing backlinks
- **44% of ChatGPT citations** come from the first third of content — front-load key claims

Sources: [ZipTie.dev — Original Research and AI Citations](https://ziptie.dev/blog/how-original-research-wins-ai-citations/), [ALM Corp — ChatGPT Citations Study](https://almcorp.com/blog/chatgpt-citations-study-44-percent-first-third-content/), [The Digital Bloom — 2025 AI Visibility Report](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/)

### What Video Types Get Cited

By AI citation frequency:

| Content Type                          | Share of Citations | Notes                     |
| ------------------------------------- | ------------------ | ------------------------- |
| Instructional / how-to                | **35.6%**          | Highest performer         |
| Visual demos / product walkthroughs   | **32.5%**          | Growing fastest           |

YouTube **rarely** gets cited for: strategic planning, career advice, or abstract concepts. It dominates for: tutorials, product demos, medical education (visual requirement), pricing comparisons, tool walkthroughs.

YouTube typically ranks **positions 3-10** within AI responses — serving as supporting evidence rather than primary source. This creates an opportunity: brands that have both strong editorial authority (text) and YouTube presence get cited twice in the same response.

### How YouTube Feeds AI Models

The mechanism is dual:

1. **Direct indexing** — YouTube transcripts, titles, descriptions, and chapter markers are indexed by Google and accessible to its AI Overviews
2. **Fan-out queries** — When Google AI generates sub-queries from the original search, YouTube URLs frequently surface as authoritative sources for the expanded topic set

Brand mentions in **video titles, transcripts, and descriptions** are the strongest correlating factor with AI Overview visibility among all studied signals.

### YouTube SEO Specifically for AI Citation

**Transcript optimisation (most important):**

- Upload manual .srt transcripts rather than relying on auto-captions (auto accuracy: 85-92%)
- Transcripts convert spoken content into indexable text — without them, AI sees only metadata
- Include keyword-specific terminology, product names, and step-by-step instructions accurately
- Videos with comprehensive transcription see **40-60% increase in organic traffic** within 6 months

**Title and description format:**

- Design titles around natural-language questions (AI responds to question-format queries)
- Answer the core question in the first 15 seconds of the video
- Use video chapters with at least 3-5 markers using keyword-focused titles

**Optimal video length:**

- **8-12 minutes** performs best for search and AI citation
- 10 minutes of clear structured explanation ranks better than 2 minutes of vague content
- AI prefers in-depth, well-structured sources it can trust

**Structural requirements:**

- Answer a key question clearly within the first 30 seconds
- Use numbered lists and clearly-defined segments
- Add schema markup (VideoObject) for enhanced machine-readability

Sources: [Search Engine Land — YouTube No Longer Optional for SEO](https://searchengineland.com/youtube-seo-ai-overviews-467253), [VdoCipher — Video SEO Best Practices 2026](https://www.vdocipher.com/blog/video-seo-best-practices/), [SEO-Marketing.koeln — YouTube SEO 2026](https://seo-marketing.koeln/en/youtube-seo-2026-how-video-chapters-and-transcripts-secure-your-google-rankings/)

### Automation Opportunity

- Auto-transcription + cleaning of uploaded videos
- Chapter marker generation from transcript content
- Description optimisation to embed natural-language questions
- Schema markup injection (VideoObject) post-upload

Source: [Britopian — How Influential is Substack in Generative Engines](https://www.britopian.com/geo/substack-generative-engines/) (published January 2026, Michael Brito, Global Head of Data + Intelligence, Zeno Group)

### Why Substack Underperforms

- Paywalls block AI crawler access on most premium content
- Fragmented subdomains (newsletter.substack.com) dilute domain authority signals
- Limited search accessibility vs. indexed open web
- The Substack writers who _do_ earn citations are predominantly former NYT, Bloomberg, Atlantic journalists — AI training data already recognised their bylines as authoritative

### Medium's SEO and AI Value in 2025-2026

- Medium pages rank in Google (domain authority strong)
- Open articles are fully crawlable
- Content appears in AI training datasets (older Medium articles especially)
- Medium still useful for distribution and referral, but less so for direct AI citation
- Cross-publishing from your main domain to Medium: set the **canonical URL** to your primary domain to preserve SEO equity — do not let Medium canonicalise to their domain

### Best Practices for Both Platforms

- **Do publish on Medium** if your content strategy allows: the DR authority helps referral and there is marginal AI citation value
- **Substack is primarily for audience building**, not AI authority — treat it as an email list, not an AI citation channel
- On Medium, enable **Friend Links** for paywalled pieces to ensure AI crawlers can access content
- Prioritise expert bylines — platforms citing "former [publication] journalist" get meaningfully more citations

## 4. Guest Posting in the AI Era {#4-guest-posting}

### Has Guest Posting Changed?

Yes, substantially. The shift from Google's traditional backlink model to AI's entity-and-authority model means:

- Guest posts on **high-DR sites increase AI visibility by 60%** (measured impact)
- **Quality over quantity**: the November 2024 Site Reputation Abuse policy means high-volume guest posting on thin-authority sites now carries Google penalty risk AND fails to move AI citation needles
- AI models value guest posts **differently** from Google — they care about whether the host publication is itself cited in AI responses (authority transference)

Source: [Organic Media Group — Guest Posting for LLM Visibility](https://organicmediagroup.org/how-to-use-guest-posting-to-get-visibility-in-llms/), [Yotpo — Guest Post Strategy for AI Overviews](https://www.yotpo.com/blog/guest-posts-strategy-ai-overviews/)

### What AI Models Look For in Guest Posts

**Site selection (most important factor):**

- Target publications that are themselves frequently cited in AI responses (run test prompts to check)
- Industry verticals: trades, professional journals, established news publications
- DA 60+ is minimum threshold worth pursuing for AI citation impact
- Publications with their own strong citation presence in Perplexity, ChatGPT, and Google AIO

**Content format for AI citation:**

- Forrester Research guidance: content must be "bold, direct, and quotable"
- Structure: include at least 2 proprietary statistics or original data points
- Use 2.5x more bullet points than standard editorial prose
- Lead with the primary insight — 44% of citations come from the first third
- Include specific expert quotes with name + credential attribution

**Topic selection:**

- Guest posts on topics where AI generates sub-queries (how-to, comparison, definition)
- Avoid opinion pieces for AI citation — favour factual, instructional, or data-heavy content

## 5. Academic and Research Distribution {#5-academic}

### How Academic Platforms Feed AI Models

AI language models were trained on large academic corpora. Academic content carries very high credibility signals in AI citation logic because:

- Peer-reviewed sources minimise AI "hallucination risk" (the AI's core selection criterion)
- DOI-linked papers are indexed in structured metadata systems AI can access with high confidence
- Open-access papers appear in Common Crawl and other large web training datasets

### Platform Assessment

| Platform            | AI Citation Value                | Access Model          | Best For                                  |
| ------------------- | -------------------------------- | --------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| **arXiv**           | High (heavily indexed)           | Open access           | Tech, AI, data science brands             |
| **ResearchGate**    | Medium (profile + paper hosting) | Partial open          | Any brand with research                   |
| **Academia.edu**    | Medium                           | Freemium              | Humanities, social science adjacent       |
| **SSRN**            | High (law, finance, economics)   | Open access           | Finance, legal, healthcare brands         |
| **medRxiv/bioRxiv** | High in health queries           | Open access preprints | Health/biotech brands with clinical data  |
| **Zenodo** (CERN)   | High (DOI-assigned, open)        | Open access           | Any brand — best for brand-owned research |
| **OSF Preprints**   | Medium-high                      | Open access           | Social science, psychology, education     |

**Critical 2025 development:** arXiv announced in November 2025 that it will **no longer accept computer science review articles** and position papers not vetted by a journal or conference — due to AI-generated content flooding the platform. Quality bar is rising.

### Practical Strategy for Non-Academic Brands

Brands (like Hairgenetix with clinical data, or agencies with survey data) can legitimately use:

1. **Zenodo** — CERN's open-access repository, issues DOIs, fully indexed, accepts any research dataset or report. Free. Best option for non-academic brands publishing proprietary data.
2. **SSRN** — accepts working papers and pre-peer-review research across business, finance, and social science. No peer review required.
3. **OSF (Open Science Framework)** — accepts pre-registrations and data from any researcher. Issues DOIs.

### Best Practices

- Publish data reports as open-access documents with a DOI assigned (even via Zenodo — it's free)
- Link your DOI-assigned paper from your main website
- Include methodology section (increases AI citation confidence)
- Open-access work is **cited more frequently** than paywalled equivalents
- Do not rely solely on your domain — distribute across 2-3 academic repositories for redundancy

Sources: [Harvard Library — Preprints Guide](https://guides.library.harvard.edu/c.php?g=1097539&p=8004417), [DeSci Labs — Preprints, Open Access and AI](https://descilabs.medium.com/preprints-open-access-and-ai-modern-publishing-for-scientists-70cbcd3ea15d), [Johns Hopkins Library — Open Access and AI Considerations](https://www.library.jhu.edu/news/2025/10/open-access-publishing-and-ai-considerations-for-authors/)

## 6. Podcast Appearances for AI Visibility {#6-podcasts}

### The Core Mechanism

Podcast audio alone is **not indexed by AI systems** — they cannot process raw audio files. The citation mechanism works entirely through text:

- Published transcripts (manually uploaded or auto-generated)
- Show notes (limited indexable content)
- Third-party transcript databases (Spotify transcripts, Apple Podcasts descriptions)

Without a transcript, a podcast appearance contributes zero to AI citation potential.

## 8. Infographics and Visual Content {#8-visual-content}

### The Honest Reality

Current AI systems are **text-first** when it comes to citations. Visual content (infographics, images) is not directly cited by AI models in the same way text sources are. However, visual content distribution contributes to AI visibility through indirect mechanisms:

1. **Alt-text and surrounding text** — the text context around an image is indexed and can be cited
2. **Pages that contain infographics** tend to attract backlinks, increasing domain authority, which feeds AI citation
3. **Pinterest and SlideShare** are not meaningfully cited in AI responses based on current data
4. **Structured data-rich visual content** (infographics with embedded schema markup) is more machine-readable

### Where Visual Content Matters for AISO

- Schema markup (ImageObject, Dataset) on visual content pages makes them more citation-friendly
- Infographics that contain **embedded statistics** often earn editorial coverage that then gets cited
- The primary distribution value of infographics is **link acquisition**, which indirectly feeds AI citation signals
- Wikipedia will embed well-designed infographics — and Wikipedia is heavily cited by all AI models

### Platform Assessment for Visual Distribution

| Platform                 | Direct AI Citation Value   | Indirect Value          | Verdict                |
| ------------------------ | -------------------------- | ----------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Pinterest                | Very low                   | Low                     | Skip for AI visibility |
| SlideShare               | Low                        | Medium (LinkedIn reach) | Minor                  |
| Canva (public designs)   | Very low                   | Low                     | Skip                   |
| Wikipedia (embedded)     | **High**                   | High                    | Target if topical      |
| Your own domain (schema) | High                       | High                    | Priority               |
| Industry publications    | High (via editorial embed) | High                    | Best indirect route    |

### Best Practice

Do not build a visual content distribution workflow for AI visibility as a standalone initiative. Instead:

- Publish infographics on your own domain with full schema markup
- Pursue editorial embedding in publications already cited in AI responses
- Use infographics as **link bait** — the editorial links they generate are the actual AI signal

Source: [Group SJR — GEO, ARO, AISO Explained](https://www.groupsjr.com/ideas/geo-aro-aiso-what-does-it-all-mean/)

## 9. Content Syndication Networks {#9-syndication}

### Outbrain and Taboola for AI Visibility

**Short verdict: Not a meaningful channel for AI citation.**

Outbrain and Taboola are native advertising networks (paid placement of content recommendations on publisher sites). They do not contribute to AI citation in any meaningful, verified way because:

- Syndicated content via these networks is typically labelled as sponsored content
- AI models primarily cite organic editorial content, not paid native placements
- The 94% non-paid source dominance in AI citations confirms this

These platforms are useful for **traffic generation** and **audience reach** but should not be positioned as AI visibility tools.

### What Does Work in Syndication

True editorial syndication (republishing with credit by authoritative publishers) does contribute to AI visibility because:

- Multiple authoritative sources referencing the same original content strengthens entity signals
- Syndicated articles on high-DR publications (Forbes, Inc, HuffPost) carry citation weight
- AP and Reuters syndication is extremely high-value for AI citation (both are top-cited by every AI model)

**Actionable:** The value is in **earned editorial syndication**, not paid distribution networks. Wire services (Topic 7) deliver better results.

Sources: [Blockchain-Ads — Taboola vs Outbrain](https://www.blockchain-ads.com/post/taboola-vs-outbrain), [Capterra — Outbrain vs Taboola](https://www.capterra.com/ppc-software/compare/140446-156009/features)

## 11. White Papers and Original Research {#11-original-research}

### Why Original Research Gets Cited Disproportionately

AI systems cite original research because it minimises their risk — a proprietary statistic from a named study is verifiable, attributable, and quotable. The citation logic is:

"What's the safest thing I can repeat without being wrong?"

Branded original research answers this perfectly: one source, one number, one attribution.

### Performance Data

| Optimisation Technique                 | AI Visibility Lift               |
| -------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Adding statistics (any)                | **+22%**                         |
| Adding original proprietary statistics | **+41%**                         |
| Adding expert quotations               | **+37%**                         |
| Listicle format                        | 25% AI citation rate             |
| Educational page format                | 19.4% of ChatGPT citations       |
| Data-rich sites vs. directory sites    | **4.31x more citations per URL** |

Source: [ZipTie.dev — Original Research Wins AI Citations](https://ziptie.dev/blog/how-original-research-wins-ai-citations/), [Presence AI — AI Citation Rates Research](https://presenceai.app/blog/ai-search-citation-rates-research-which-content-gets-cited)

### Publication Strategy for Maximum AI Citation

**Layered distribution (proven model):**

1. **Primary comprehensive report** — hosted on your domain with full schema markup (Dataset, Article)
2. **Vertical breakdowns** — segment-specific analyses (e.g., "Hair Loss in Women Over 40" as standalone page)
3. **Methodology documentation** — published separately, AI cites methodology sections specifically
4. **FAQ content** — addressing sub-queries the primary research generates
5. **Cross-platform distribution** — same research filed in:
   - Zenodo (DOI, open access) — free
   - SSRN (if business/finance/health relevance)
   - Academia.edu (free tier)
   - ResearchGate (if author has academic affiliation or co-author does)

**DOI Registration:**

- Zenodo (CERN) issues DOIs for free for any research dataset or report
- DOI-linked content is treated with higher credibility by AI models
- Process: upload PDF to Zenodo → select "Open Access" → submit → receive DOI within minutes

**Community distribution:**

- Reddit (relevant subreddits) — Reddit is heavily indexed and cited
- Wikipedia (where data supports existing articles — cite your DOI)
- Industry forums and community platforms

Source: [ZipTie.dev — Original Research Wins AI Citations](https://ziptie.dev/blog/how-original-research-wins-ai-citations/)

### Specific Application: Hairgenetix

Hairgenetix has clinical data on peptide hair growth efficacy. This is exactly the type of original research that:

- Gets cited in health-adjacent AI queries ("does [peptide] work for hair loss")
- Can be filed on medRxiv/bioRxiv (preprints) if clinical in nature
- Can be DOI-registered on Zenodo even without a peer review requirement
- Should be written in both scientific and lay formats (two separate publications)
